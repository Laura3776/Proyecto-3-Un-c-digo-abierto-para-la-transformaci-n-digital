Overview

How to open the app: click Open SmartLog Monitor above. Once inside, select a user from the dropdown and click Log in.

SmartLog Monitor is a web application for managing operational incidents with role-based access control: reporter, technician, supervisor, auditor, and administrator.

The system centralizes incident creation, assignment, tracking, closure, audit, and reporting — eliminating siloed work and improving response times.

The application is designed for daily operations: each user logs in with their profile and sees only the panels, filters, and actions permitted for their role. This reduces process errors and improves traceability.

  • Problem it solves: incidents scattered across chats, emails, and spreadsheets with no unified control.
  • Expected outcome: a single, measurable, auditable flow from creation to closure.
  • Target environment: IT and OT teams that need operational coordination and compliance.
SmartLog Monitor applies role-based management with separation of responsibilities, full traceability, and data lifecycle control.

Functional Architecture

  • Main interface: role-based panels and work views (lists, dashboards, logs, reports).
  • Incident engine: state machine, assignments, priorities, and deadlines.
  • Traceability: action log for audit and tracking.
  • Governance: user, role, permission, and system parameter management.

Functional building blocks

  • Session and profile: user selection, login/logout, personal data, password, and notification preferences.
  • Incident management: creation, filters, detail view, comments, attachments, history, and state changes.
  • Team coordination: supervisor assignment, workload balancing, team views, and Kanban board.
  • Audit and control: action log, traceability by user/role, exports, and reports.
  • Administration: users, roles/permissions, areas, types, SLA parameters, and transition rules.

How an Incident Works (Step by Step)

Incident creation

The reporter creates the ticket with area, type, description, and attachments. The system validates data and generates an ID, initial state, and deadline.

Assignment

The supervisor assigns a technician from their area. The incident moves to In analysis and a traceability event is recorded.

Technical execution

The technician analyses the issue, advances the state, adds comments and evidence, and requests additional information if needed.

Closure

After resolving, the reporter validates and confirms. The final closure is recorded with the complete history.

Audit

The auditor reviews events, detects anomalies, and exports evidence for compliance and continuous improvement.

Roles and Responsibilities

The system applies RBAC control: each role sees only what it should and can only execute permitted actions.

Role Visibility Main responsibility Key actions
Reporter Own incidents only Create incidents and provide follow-up information Create, view detail, comment, attach, confirm closure
Technician Team or assigned incidents Analyse and resolve technical incidents Change state, comment, upload evidence, request reassignment
Supervisor Area incidents Operational flow control and SLA compliance Assign technician, change priority/category, close/reopen, reports
Auditor Global read-only Verify traceability and compliance View incidents, global log, reports, audit export
Administrator Global system Platform governance and configuration Manage users, roles, permissions, areas, types, parameters, logs

Operational detail per role

Reporter: creates incidents, views "My incidents", adds comments and attachments, and confirms closure when the solution is valid.

Technician: works their assigned queue, advances states, requests information from the reporter, and adds technical evidence of the work done.

Supervisor: distributes workload among technicians, prioritizes by impact, controls SLA, can close/reopen, and monitors operational health of the area.

Auditor: does not operate incidents, but reviews global logs, users, reports, and exports traceability and compliance evidence.

Administrator: maintains the system: users, policies, permissions, area/type catalogue, priority rules, and transitions.

Screens by Role

Expand each role to see its screens, actions, and limits.

Reporter
  • Screens: Create incident, My incidents, Detail, My profile.
  • Can do: submit a ticket, filter own incidents, comment, attach files, confirm closure.
  • Cannot do: assign technicians or change global priority.
Technician
  • Screens: Dashboard, Assigned, Team, Kanban, Calendar, Chat, My profile.
  • Can do: advance permitted states, add evidence, comment, request reassignment.
  • Cannot do: modify permissions or administrative configuration.
Supervisor
  • Screens: Dashboard, Incidents, Assignment, Team workload, Area Kanban, Reports, Chat, Calendar.
  • Can do: assign technicians, prioritize, control SLA, close/reopen, coordinate the area.
  • Cannot do: administer global system roles.
Auditor
  • Screens: Audit panel, Incidents, Global log, Reports, Users, My profile.
  • Can do: review traceability, filter events, detect risks, export evidence.
  • Cannot do: operate incidents (read-only mode).
Administrator
  • Screens: Admin panel, Users, Roles/permissions, Configuration, Incidents, Logs, Monitoring.
  • Can do: govern users, policies, catalogues, parameters, and system rules.
  • Should not do: replace the daily operations of supervisor and technician.

Incident State Machine

States are not free: the system validates permitted transitions to maintain data consistency.

New In analysis In progress Pending information Resolved Closed
  • New: created but not yet assigned to a technician.
  • In analysis: assigned technician reviewing cause and scope.
  • In progress: corrective work underway.
  • Pending information: blocked waiting for data from the reporter.
  • Resolved: solution applied, pending final closure confirmation.
  • Closed: finalized — confirmed by reporter or force-closed by supervisor.
Every state change is saved with who made it, when, and the change detail — both in the incident timeline and in the global audit log.

Views and Main Tools

Tables and filters

  • Search by ID or free text.
  • Filters by state, priority, type, area, and date.
  • Operations-oriented tables with direct row actions.

Kanban and calendar

  • Kanban view for dragging incidents between valid states.
  • Calendar view for controlling deadlines and temporal workload.

Internal chat and alerts

  • Coordination channels per technical/supervisor team.
  • Usage policy: do not share sensitive data in messages.

KPIs and reports

  • Indicators for open, pending, resolved, overdue, and SLA compliance.
  • Dashboards for daily tracking and weekly reviews.

Full Real-World Example

  1. Reporter opens INC-1009 for a network failure in OT area with high priority.
  2. OT area supervisor assigns tec-paula — incident moves to In analysis.
  3. Technician diagnoses, changes to In progress, adds evidence and technical comment.
  4. Issue is resolved, state changes to Resolved — reporter is notified.
  5. Reporter validates and confirms: final state Closed.
  6. Auditor verifies that the full chain has complete traceability (no gaps).
This flow summarises the real logic of the application: every step is recorded by user, role, timestamp, and action.
